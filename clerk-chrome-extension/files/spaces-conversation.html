This is a juicy one. The tweet articulates the vision well—the gap between "generating state" and having that state be useful later is where most people lose 90% of the value of their own thinking.

Some real tensions I'd want the spec to grapple with:

Data portability is the first wall. ChatGPT has export, Claude has export, but they're different formats with different metadata richness. Most social platforms actively fight against this.

The semantic layer is the actual product. Raw conversation logs are nearly useless. The value is in the contextualization—linking your half-formed idea from a Claude chat in March to the thread you posted in September.

Resurfacing mechanics define the UX. Push (proactive "you talked about this before") vs. pull (search/query) are completely different products.
